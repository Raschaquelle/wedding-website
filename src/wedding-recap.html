Wedding Recap

Date: June 4th, 2021
Location: Little Molas Lake Overlook
Hiking Up
The wedding ceremony was held just off the Colorado Trail about 2 miles above Little Molas Lake. We scouted the spot the day before and set up a makeshift aisle with rocks and sticks. When we went to do this however, the gate to the trailhead was still locked for the season and Forest Service workers were doing some maintenance at the trailhead. We told the workers that we were getting married there the next day and that we’d really appreciate it if they could open the trailhead (it was supposed to open for the season on June 1st). They said they were 95% sure they would be able to open it in time and luckily when we called the ranger the morning of the wedding, he gave us the good news that the trailhead was open!

Everyone suited up and put on sunscreen at the trailhead and then we began the 2 mile hike up the trail. The weather was spectacular and we had a nice leisurely hike, enjoying the scenery and getting lots of pictures. When the trail reached the plateau near the ceremony location we laid down some flags to guide the guests off of the trail and to the special spot. When we arrived at the spot we set up a tent for guests to change in (and for Rachelle to touch up her make-up and hair). Once everyone had changed and put on sunscreen again, we began the ceremony.
❮ ❯

Ceremony
The ceremony was short and simple and went off without a hitch. Rachelle walked down our makeshift aisle to the sounds of nature. Our close friend Duncan served as our officiant and opened the ceremony by thanking everyone for attending and telling a few stories about us. This was followed by a beautiful reading delivered by Ben’s mom. At this point, Rachelle’s close friend Phil brought up a stone with a USGS replica marker embedded in it. This marker included the exact GPS coordinates of the ceremony, the elevation, and our names. We had this marker made to commemorate this special occasion and mark this permanent milestone in our relationship. Duncan placed the stone on the ground and asked us to step forward so that we faced each other over the stone. We then recited our vows that we each wrote ourselves and then exchanged rings. Duncan pronounced us officially married, we shared our first kiss, and walked down the aisle hand in hand.

After the ceremony concluded, we took lots of photos and enjoyed a nice picnic lunch. To cap everything off we popped a bottle of sparkling cider and had a toast. After snapping a few more photos, everyone packed up, reapplied sunscreen, and we started the hike down.
❮ ❯

Hiking Down
For the hike down, we attached some hand-made signs to our backpacks that said “Just Married”. On the way down we collected our flags and took lots more photos. Upon reaching the trailhead, we decided to walk over to Little Molas Lake and snap a few more photos. Luckily the weather held all day and by mid-afternoon we were ready to head to the reception.
❮ ❯

Reception
Following the wedding we held a simple reception at the rental condo we were staying at near Purgatory Resort. We set up a few decor items including a hand-made wooden topographic map of the area where we got married. The map showed the exact spot where we got married and included all the mountains we could see from the wedding. We enjoyed some home-made Indian food for dinner and then gave guests an opportunity to say a few words if they would like. After everyone had a chance to share, we enjoyed a delicious dessert of hot fudge pudding cake and ice cream. We could not have asked for a better wedding day!
