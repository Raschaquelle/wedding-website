Honeymoon Backpacking Recap
Hut-to-Hut Sneffels Traverse
The day after the wedding we headed out with two of our friends to go on a 5-day backpacking trip across the Sneffels Range. Over the course of the 5 days we hiked a total of 27 miles, stopping at a different backcountry hut each night. We started on the west end of the range at the North Pole Hut and got to hike through some lovely meadows of wildflowers. The second day was our longest day where we made our way to the Blue Lakes Hut at the base of Mt. Sneffels (the only 14er in the range). Despite the long day, we arrived at the hut early so Ben and Duncan decided to hike up the Blue Lakes in the afternoon. Day 3 was our steepest ascent as we climbed up and over Wilson Creek Summit where we got some spectacular views of Mt. Sneffels. Day 4 was a short day hiking between the Ridgway Hut and Burn Hut, so we spent the afternoon at the Burn Hut reading and hiking around the hut. The final day was a short and steep descent down to our car at the East Dallas Trailhead just north of Ouray. We had perfect weather for the entire trip and it was such a fantastic way to celebrate our marriage!
