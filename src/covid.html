Covid-related Information for Ben + Rachelle's Wedding
See May 2021 Updates in red
Our goal is to have an intimate wedding and a fun post wedding backpacking trip that is as close to normal as possible. In order to make that happen we are taking COVID-19 very seriously and instituting a set of precautions to allow us to have a small gathering of people in person without masks and social distancing. It is only by following these precautions and continuing to monitor the evolving COVID-19 situation that we can make this wedding as safe as possible. All that said, there is no such thing as “zero risk” and we feel that it is important to make you aware of the fact that there will be a certain amount of risk associated with attending the wedding.
Wedding Details
The current guest list includes 11 people (including Ben and Rachelle). The wedding itself will be held outdoors along the Colorado Trail. The reception will be held indoors at the lodging location. Weddings have been shown to be super-spreader events so we are doing our best to make sure that doesn’t happen. We have a responsibility:
  • to ourselves to make sure this joyous occasion is as safe as possible
  • to you all to make sure precautions are in place so that you can attend safely
  • and to our local communities to hold the event with appropriate precautions to reduce the risk of community spread
We are placing our trust in you all to read through the following information in detail, follow the requested precautions, and communicate openly with us about your concerns and questions. We are not medical experts and are basing our recommendations on the latest information from the CDC and Colorado Department of Health and doing our best to balance the risks and benefits.
May 2021 Update: The situation in Colorado and throughout the U.S. has greatly improved thanks to the wide distribution of vaccines. That said, cases are still high in some areas so we want to continue to be cautious and responsible. What this means for you is that the original precautions and requirements are still in effect, but that the overall risk level has gone down significantly.
COVID-19 Precaution Requirements
Generally the requirements are to self-isolate prior to traveling and to wear masks and practice social distancing in public places. We do not plan on having people wear masks or social distance during the ceremony or at the lodging place.

Travel Requirements:
  1. Quarantine/Self-Isolate for 14 days prior to attending the wedding (this time can include travel time)
    1. This means to stay home except for essential activities which are listed below.
      • Essential errands such as getting groceries, medicine, etc. are totally fine as long as you wear a mask and the establishment has a mask policy. Note: Going to the gym and going out to eat are NOT considered essential activities.
      • Going to work is totally fine if your work requires you to be in person and has a strict COVID-19 policy including mask wearing, frequent sanitation, capacity limitations, etc.
    2. The CDC is currently still recommending a 14 day quarantine if you have potentially been exposed (via close contact) to COVID-19. A shorter quarantine of 7 to 10 days is acceptable under certain circumstances such as a negative test result
      • May 2021 Update: If you have been fully vaccinated the CDC does not recommend a quarantine if you have potentially been exposed to Covid-19. That said, we still ask that you self-isolate for 14 days prior to the wedding.
  2. Wear masks and practice social distancing during your travels and when in any public place (i.e. rest stops, gas stations) May 2021 Update:This is especially important as many places are now lifting mask orders and other COVID-19 related public health orders.
  3. Travel by private transportation (i.e. your personal vehicle).
    1. Please follow the CDC’s travel guidelines (also here) when preparing to attend the wedding
  4. Lodging during travel should be one of the following:
    1. Campground/Dispersed Camping
    2. AirBnB/Hotel/Motel if it has a mask policy, separate facilities from other guests (i.e. not a shared kitchen/bathroom in an Airbnb), and is following the proper local/state guidelines for operation
  5. Fill out this Coronavirus Self-Checker prior to departing to indicate that you have not had any symptoms in the two weeks leading up to the wedding
  6. If you are not fully vaccinated and have access to testing, consider getting tested prior to traveling
Wedding Requirements:
  1. Stay at the provided lodging with all other guests (this self-isolates all wedding guests)
  2. Wear masks when out in indoor public places (i.e. visiting stores, using a public restroom, etc.)
  3. Practice appropriate personal hygiene including frequent hand washing with soap and water
May 2021 Update: The statewide mask mandate in Colorado is still currently in effect but only applies to indoor public spaces and in cases where less than 80% of the people in the indoor space are not vaccinated. For this reason, we are lifting the previous requirement of needing to wear masks while hiking to the wedding ceremony or interacting with the photographer. We have confirmed that the photographer is fully vaccinated.
Why the Precautions?
We want to have guests be able to stay together and be able to have an indoor reception after the wedding. During the course of the wedding festivities we want people to be able to be in close physical contact (i.e. hugging, handshakes) and not have to wear masks when indoors with other guests. The best way to do this is to reduce the risk that guests have had contact with someone with COVID-19 prior to attending the wedding.
May 2021 Update: With the wide distribution of vaccines, the safest and most effective way to ensure that the wedding is as low risk as possible is to get vaccinated. If you have access to the vaccine and feel comfortable doing so, we highly encourage you to get the vaccine.
What About Flying?
While the risk of contracting COVID-19 during a flight is very low (also here), the CDC still advises against air travel due to the need to be in close contact with people in airports where proper social distancing and air circulation is not always possible. So while being in the actual plane is likely pretty safe, getting from your home to that plane is quite risky and we believe that risk is not worth it.
May 2021 Update: We are still not allowing guests to fly to the wedding.
What About Vaccines?
May 2021 Update: Vaccines are now widely available and we highly encourage you to get the vaccine if you have access to it and are comfortable doing so. We have confirmed that most of the people attending the wedding (including the photographer) will be fully vaccinated by the time of the wedding. This greatly reduces the risk associated with the wedding which is great news! Additionally, this means for those of you who are not vaccinated, you will be well protected by the other attendees.

Even if you are vaccinated, the CDC still recommends maintaining similar precautions such as mask wearing and social distancing. Please be respectful of other guests though and continue to follow the above listed precautions.
